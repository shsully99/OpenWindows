Introduction



Enter up to 4 noise levels on the top left of the screen, each of which represents your assessment of the free-field noise levels at the facade. Then on the right of the screen enter the room dimensions of the room under consideration.

Next on the top right of the screen enter the details of the facade element you wish to add and click the "search" button. You will be presented with matching facade elements from the database. Also shown is the calculated total noise level of you include that element in your search. Click the Add button for an element you wish to include in your calculation.

The selected element(s) will be displayed. The display also shows the contribution that element makes to the overall noise level as a decIbel level and also as a percentage. You can turn elements on or off on this screen on the Selected column or remove them by clicking the remove button on the far right of the screen.

On the centre left you will see the "Share" and "Download" links. "Share" takes you to a screen where you can copy a "Share" link into the copy/paste clipboard of your PC. Paste it into an email to send to other members of your design team who will then be able to see your design if they click on that link.

"Download" will download the calculation into an Excel spreadsheet. This allows further processing of the results including the ability to add "custom" elements. Currently this is the only place you can add a custom element.

You can "toggle" between the element display and Search screen by clicking "Show Selected" and "Search".

The calculation is carried according to two formular presented in BS 8233 and BS EN 12354-3 and shown on the right (excerpt from BB93). The results at each frequency for each element are added logarithmically to determine the overall noise level within a room.







Help for entry fields

One can enter up to four types of noise levels which the facade is exposed to.

  • The energy "average" noise levels over a 16 hour daytime period (LAeq16h)
  • The energy "average" noise levels over an 8 hour night-time period (LAeq8h)
  • The 10 highest impulsive noise level of an 8 hour night-time period (LAF,max)
  • The 1highest impulsive noise level of an 8 hour night-time period (LAF,max)
You can enter the noise level at the facade as an overall level in the left hand entry box or the spectra at each octave band from 125 Hz to 2000 Hz, sepearated by "-" or "/"/. If you enter an overall level then a spectra will be created and shaped according to the one shown in ISO 717-1 (Ctr).


Enter either
  • The volume in cubic metres (m3) OR
  • The area (m3) x height (m) OR
  • 3 dimensions (length (m) x width (m) x height (m) )
Note: If you select an open area element it will be selected as a percentage of the floor area.


From the drop-down list enter the type of element you wish to search the database for.


Enter the surface area of the element selected im m2 or equivalent area in mm2 for vents. The area entered here is used to calculate the internal noise level for each element shown when you press search. For open areas the area is the "acoustic open area". For vents a number of vents will be shown which satisfies the equivant area value. So if you ask for 10000 mm2 a vent of 4000 mm2 will be assumed to have a quantity of 3 in order to comply with your request for a minimum equivalent area of 1000 4000 mm2.


If this element is placed on a quieter facade than enter the number of deciBels quieter. This is particuarly useful if you want to estimate the effect of opening a window on a facade that does not face a noise source.


If you enter a value here then that valuee will be used to filter the search results.
